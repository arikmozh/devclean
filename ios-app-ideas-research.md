# Profitable Simple iOS App Ideas for Solo Developers
## Comprehensive Research Report (March 2026)

---

## Executive Summary

After extensive research across indie developer blogs, Indie Hackers, Hacker News, revenue-sharing posts, and App Store analysis, clear patterns emerge about what types of simple iOS apps actually make money for solo developers. The key finding: **hyper-niche utility apps with subscription monetization, built quickly and marketed through ASO, consistently outperform complex apps built by solo developers.**

## Part 2: Specific App Ideas That Work

### Category A: Habit & Productivity Trackers (HIGH POTENTIAL)

**Why it works:** People pay subscriptions for self-improvement. Health & Fitness is the fastest-growing subscription category on iOS.

1. **Habit Tracker with Visual Streaks**
   - HabitKit proves this: GitHub-style contribution grid for habits
   - Revenue: $15K-$31K/month
   - Pricing: Freemium with $3-5/month or $30-40/year subscription
   - Build time: 4-8 weeks MVP
   - Differentiation angle: Niche it down (habits for runners, habits for students, habits for new parents)

2. **Pomodoro / Focus Timer**
   - Session makes $5K/month net profit
   - Flow, Focus Keeper are successful competitors
   - Add: Focus music, distraction blocking, daily reports
   - Pricing: $2-4/month subscription

3. **Journaling App with AI Insights**
   - Day One charges subscription and has loyal users
   - Opportunity: AI-powered mood analysis, gratitude prompts, photo journals
   - Pricing: $3.99/week or $39/year
   - Emotional well-being tools growing faster than meditation apps

### Category B: Simple Utility Apps (PROVEN MODEL)

**Why it works:** Users actively search for these. High intent, low competition in sub-niches.

4. **PDF Scanner / Converter**
   - Genius Scan: profitable for 15 years, 4M monthly users, fully bootstrapped
   - Niche angles: Scanner for receipts, scanner for students, scanner for contractors
   - Monetization: Freemium + subscription ($4.99/month) for OCR, cloud sync

5. **QR Code Generator/Scanner**
   - Simple to build, constant demand
   - Premium features: Custom branded QR codes, batch generation, analytics
   - Monetization: Free with ads, $2.99 one-time or $1.99/month

6. **Unit/Currency Converter**
   - Evergreen demand, minimal maintenance
   - Niche angles: Cooking-specific converter, engineering units, currency for travelers
   - Monetization: One-time purchase $2.99-$4.99

7. **File Converter (Images, Audio, Video)**
   - Users constantly need to convert HEIC to JPG, MOV to MP4, etc.
   - Subscription model: $2.99/month for unlimited conversions
   - Very low maintenance after initial build

### Category C: Customization & Aesthetic Apps (HIGH MARGIN)

**Why it works:** iOS users love customization. Widget apps exploded after iOS 14+. Subscription models work because content can be refreshed.

8. **Widget & Wallpaper App**
   - iScreen and similar apps use aggressive paywall with yearly/lifetime plans
   - Content: Aesthetic widgets, custom icons, wallpapers
   - Pricing: $4.99/week, $9.99/month, or $39.99/year
   - Revenue potential: Very high if you nail the aesthetic
   - Key: Regular content updates justify subscription

9. **Custom Icon Pack Creator**
   - Let users create/apply custom home screen icon packs
   - Subscription for premium icon sets
   - Cross-sell wallpapers and widgets

### Category D: Health & Wellness (STRONG SUBSCRIPTION FIT)

**Why it works:** Health & Fitness has enormous user demand, strong subscription adoption, and endless sub-niches. Each sub-niche can support a profitable indie app.

10. **White Noise / Sleep Sounds**
    - Market validation: White Noise Deep Sleep Sounds does ~$200K/month estimated revenue
    - Surprisingly simple to build: audio playback + timer + mixer
    - Niche angles: Sleep sounds for babies, nature sounds, ASMR, rain sounds
    - Pricing: Freemium with $3.99/month for premium sounds

11. **Posture Correction / Reminder App**
    - Underserved niche within Health & Fitness
    - Use device sensors for posture detection
    - Subscription: $2.99-$4.99/month

12. **Water/Hydration Tracker**
    - Simple concept, highly retentive if done well
    - Apple Watch complication adds value
    - Subscription or one-time purchase

13. **Stretching / Micro-Exercise App**
    - Target desk workers
    - Timed routines with animations
    - Subscription: $3.99/month

### Category E: Finance & Tracking (USERS PAY FOR THIS)

14. **Simple Expense Tracker**
    - Dime (built by solo student developer) proves the concept
    - Budget Flow (solo developer side project) shows viability
    - Niche angles: Expense tracker for freelancers, for couples, for students
    - Pricing: One-time $4.99 or subscription $2.99/month

15. **Subscription Manager / Bill Tracker**
    - Average American pays for 9 app subscriptions ($127/year on apps)
    - Help users track and manage their subscriptions
    - Monetization: Ironic but effective subscription at $1.99/month

16. **Investment / Savings Goal Tracker**
    - Personal finance app market: $1.48B in 2025, growing 6.2% CAGR
    - Simple visual tracking of savings goals
    - Subscription: $3.99/month

### Category F: AI-Enhanced Simple Apps (2026 TREND)

17. **AI Recipe Suggester from Pantry Photos**
    - Scan fridge/pantry, get recipes from what you have
    - Uses device camera + AI API
    - Subscription: $4.99/month

18. **AI-Powered Micro-Habit Manager**
    - Analyzes phone usage patterns to find optimal habit windows
    - Machine learning for personalized suggestions
    - Subscription: $3.99/month

19. **AI Writing/Grammar Checker (Niche)**
    - Target a specific audience: students, ESL learners, legal professionals
    - Subscription: $4.99-$9.99/month

### Category G: Niche Counters & Trackers

20. **Tally Counter / Clicker**
    - Iconfactory's Clicker has hundreds of thousands of users
    - Extremely simple to build
    - Monetization: Free with premium ($2.99) for widgets, Apple Watch, unlimited counters
    - Niche angles: Knitting row counter, bird watching counter, inventory counter

21. **Pet Care Tracker**
    - Track feeding, medications, vet visits, walks
    - Underserved niche with passionate audience
    - Subscription: $2.99/month

22. **Plant Care / Watering Reminder**
    - Track watering schedules, sunlight needs, fertilizing
    - Photo journal of plant growth
    - Subscription: $2.99/month

## Part 4: Strategy Insights from Successful Developers

### The Portfolio Strategy (Recommended)
- Build multiple small apps (4-8 week MVPs) rather than one big app
- Max Artemov: 30 apps to $22K/month in under a year
- Adam Lyttle: 50+ apps to $50K/month
- "One app is a gamble. Multiple apps are a strategy."
- Each app targets a different keyword/niche
- Combined revenue creates financial stability

### ASO-First Development
- "Find a keyword with high popularity and low difficulty (popularity >20, difficulty <60). Build the app around that keyword." - Max Artemov
- 98% of HabitKit users find it directly on the App Store
- Organic discovery through ASO is the #1 growth channel for indie apps

### The $500K Niche Rule
- "A market worth $500K/year is not worth a big company's time. But for a solo developer, $500K/year is life-changing money."
- Target niches too small for VC-backed companies but perfect for one person

### Monetization Hierarchy (Best to Worst for Solo Devs)
1. **Subscriptions** - Recurring revenue, most stable ($2-5/month sweet spot)
2. **One-time purchase** - Simple, no churn worry ($2.99-$9.99)
3. **Freemium + lifetime purchase** - Good for utility apps ($9.99-$29.99)
4. **Ads** - Only works at scale (100K+ DAU), but can work in portfolio approach

### Marketing That Works
- **App Store Optimization (ASO):** Title, subtitle, keywords, screenshots
- **Apple Search Ads:** Start at $5/day, highly targeted
- **Building in public:** Share progress on Twitter/X (worked for HabitKit)
- **Product Hunt launches**
- Social media engagement (not spam, genuine community)

### Critical Success Factors
1. **Validate before building** - Check search volume, look at competitor reviews for gaps
2. **Plan monetization from day one** - Don't launch free and figure it out later
3. **UX over code quality** - Users don't care about clean code; they care about speed and simplicity
4. **Track retention metrics** - 1-day, 7-day, 30-day retention; conversion rates
5. **Iterate, don't quit** - Most devs quit too early. Small improvements compound.
6. **Marketing beats code** - A great app nobody knows about is invisible
